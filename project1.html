Enhancing Student Experience with M-Job Hunt

Background

In the academic world, on-campus employment has become an essential need for college students. Gaining work experience and earning extra income while pursuing a degree is a top priority. However, securing on-campus part-time jobs has proven to be a challenging endeavor. At the University of Michigan, there is currently no dedicated platform for on-campus part-time job listings.

Problem Statement

How can students view and apply for part-time jobs which are listed on-campus across all departments and schools.

Challenges of Current System
  1. Lack of Categorization: Job listings lack proper categorization, making it difficult for students to find the specific job they are seeking.
  2. Tedious Job Tracking: The current process relies on students tracking job postings through email, a cumbersome task that often leads to neglect or forgotten opportunities.
  3. Transparency and Response Time: The current system lacks transparency, with extended response wait times, resulting in students losing interest in the job application process.
Solution

The proposed solution is an interactive application, "M Job Hunt," designed to help students efficiently discover on-campus job opportunities in one centralized location. This tool empowers students to view job listings, apply for positions, and manage their applications at their convenience, reducing stress and improving transparency.

Development Process

  • 1

    NeedFinding

    The project commenced with market research to understand how students currently discover on-campus job opportunities. The research revealed that 55% of students rely on email or face-to-face interactions, and 49% of institutions use new student orientation to showcase job options. Interviews with students were also conducted to comprehend their motivations and experiences related to on-campus employment.

  • 2

    Analysis

    The design process involved creating personas based on key research findings. Two primary personas emerged: students and employers. This step facilitated the development of user stories, user flow, and the Question of Change (QOC) diagram, which aided in organizing ideas, aligning them with user needs, addressing the problem, and achieving the design goals.

  • 3

    Ideation

    The ideation process began with storyboarding to visualize the design solution's overarching vision. User flow diagrams were developed to outline the application's logic, ensuring all features and interactions were well-defined before prototyping.

  • 4

    Low-Mid Fidelity Design

    Initial design concepts were sketched out using pencil and paper to establish the visual appearance and interaction flow of the application. These sketches were then transformed into an interactive low-fidelity prototype using Figma.

  • 5

    Micro Usability Testing

    To enhance the user experience, three rounds of micro-usability tests were conducted with peers. Participants were tasked with completing various actions, and their feedback was collected using a rating scale. The average scores identified areas in need of improvement.

  • 6

    High Fidelity Prototype

    In response to micro-usability test feedback, the high-fidelity prototype was refined. Notable additions included an "Interest Board" for students to share their interests with employers and a forum for students to gain insights about jobs from former student employees.

Impact
The "M-Job Hunt" project will has a transformative impact on the University of Michigan community, both for students and employers seeking on-campus part-time jobs. The following key impacts can be highlighted:
  1. 1. Enhanced Transparency: Students can now monitor the status of their applications, reducing uncertainty and frustration. This has led to a more engaging and positive experience for all users.
  2. 2. Streamlined Job Discovery: By centralizing job listings and providing an easy-to-navigate platform, students now have a convenient and efficient way to explore and apply for jobs across all university departments and schools.
  3. 3. Improved User Experience: Students can now easily search for jobs based on their preferences and track their applications. Employers can efficiently post openings, engage with potential candidates, and receive valuable student feedback.
  4. 4. Efficient Communication: The platform fosters better communication between students and potential employers. The addition of features like the "Interest Board" and the job-related forum has created a space for students to showcase their interests and gain insights about job opportunities from former student employees.
Future Steps
To further enhance M Job Hunt and make it even more valuable tool for students, employers, and the University of Michigan community as a whole, the next steps include:
  1. 1. Incorporating enhanced physical device accessibility features such as voice command, multiple screen options, and physical icons.
  2. 2. Expanding the range of size options to accommodate diverse living spaces and aesthetic preferences.
  3. 3. Introducing additional notification feeder to increase flexibility and suitability for various college configurations.
