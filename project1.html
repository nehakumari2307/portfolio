M Job Hunt

Interaction Design Project

Background

On-campus employment has become the need of the hour and a major ask from college students. To gain experience and earn extra income, students are looking for ways to secure part-time jobs on campus, which is difficult to achieve in current scenarios. There isn't any platform available at the University of Michigan which is dedicated to on-campus part-time jobs.

Problem Statement

How can students view and apply for part-time jobs which are listed on-campus across all departments and schools.

Challenges of Current System
  1. 1. Tracking email for every job posting is a tedious task, which often results in being neglected or forgotten.
  2. 2. No proper job categorization makes it hard for students to find the job they are looking for.
  3. 3. No transparency in the process with high response wait time results in students losing interest.
Solution

Provide an interactive app that helps students find listed on-campus jobs at one place. With this tool, students can view jobs; apply to them, and track them at their convenience, thus, making their life less stressful and giving more transparency.

Development Process

  • 1

    NeedFinding

    Performed Market research was performed to understand the importance and how students come across on-campus job. According to the stats, 55% directly reached out by email or face-to-face, and about 49% of institutions used new student orientation to showcase job options. Interviewing students to understand their intentions and journey behind on-campus jobs was also part of the research study.

  • 2

    Analysis

    The process started with persona creation that reflected key research points. The primary persona(students) and the served persona(an employer) were two kinds of personas which got created. This process also helped me propel my story mapping and user flow later in the design process. After designing personas and figuring out user needs, the QOC diagram was an effective way to organize and visualize which idea would meet the user's needs, address the problem, and meet the design goal.

  • 3

    Ideation

    The process started with storyboarding to comprehend the vision of the design solution. Later, the user flow diagram allowed me to work out the logic of my application before prototyping, which painted a clear picture of all features/ interactions the tool should have before prototyping.

  • 4

    Low-Mid Fidelity Design

    Process started with pencil sketches to layout the visual appearance and interaction flow of the application. Thereafter, converted those sketches into an interactive low-fidelity prototype on Figma.

  • 5

    Micro Usability Testing

    To improve the user experience, I conducted three micro-usability tests with peers. Each participant received a sheet with five tasks and a rating scale of 1 to 5. The average of all participant's scores highlighted which area of the tool required more work.

  • 6

    High Fidelity Prototype

    Based on Micro-Usability feedback, the design for the high-fidelity prototype of the application had to undergo some tweaks. Integrated some 'WoW Features' such as an Interest board, which helps students to share their interests for employers to see, and a forum that provides a platform to learn more about a job from ex-student employees.
