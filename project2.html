Ultimate Cat Feeder

Pervasive Interaction Design Project

Background

The pet-care market lacks comprehensive health monitoring and management products for cats, resulting in owners relying on vets for all their questions. Additionally, cat owners lack knowledge of the consequences of overfeeding and the different types of diseases that are related to overfeeding.

Problem Statement

Support cat owners’ desire and effort to track and understand their cats’ habits and health metrics while creating a safe space for their cat to eat.

Solution

An automated IoT device that measures and tracks key cat health metrics like weight, food, and water intake while limiting feeder access to authorized pets via a sensor. Cat owners can customize their experience and indicate when and how much their cat(s) should eat. Over time, the feeder collects pertinent information to show owners data on their pet’s eating and drinking habits - including changes and potential adjustments.

Challenges
  1. 1. With many cat feeder devices available on market, creating a new cat device had to stand out and should serve more than scheduling feeding time.
  2. 2. Changing old habits of the owner and re-training pets to start something new is tough, and many times owners want to stick to their schedule.
  3. 3. Identifying cat owners who are willing to participate in the study and let us monitor their relationship with their cats to gain a deeper understanding of the interaction.

Development Process

  • 1

    Research Study & Findings

    To ground our understanding of cats’ feeding habits, owner concerns, and other issues they face regarding their cats, we conducted a 4-day diary study with five volunteers who were primary caretakers. During this study, participants outlined their interactions with their pets and feelings about their health, and the obstacles they faced as cat owners. To supplement and support our diary study results, we surveyed 39 cat owners with cats ranging from kitten to senior age.

  • 2

    User Enactments Study

    A speed dating matrix was developed with three dimensions and five scenarios based on the feasibility and reasonableness of implementing those tasks. The selected speed dating scenarios were then converted into actionable tasks using props and were then user tested.

  • 3

    System Prototype

    Based on user test feedback, a system-level design was sketched with all the features which covered the user's needs. Among the components of the finalized system proposal were a cat house with a weighted food bowl that would monitor food intake, a pet weight scale to monitor cat weight, a water level monitor to monitor cat hydration, and an integrated camera detecting the presence of a designated animal to open the door when it senses the animal.

  • 4

    Wizard Of Oz

    Given the complexity of the device, it would take an extended time and money to develop a fully functioning, high-fidelity prototype. The 'Wizard of Oz' technique was used to demonstrate our product to the audience using easy-to-get and cheap materials. To automate the proposed device we integrated an IOT component that indicated when its was food time, when food quantity is low and when other pets try get in.
