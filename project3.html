Enhancing Digital Steward Training for the Detroit Community Technology Project

Background

The Detroit Community Technology Project (DCTP) is committed to addressing the significant digital inequality in underserved Detroit neighborhoods through its Equitable Internet Initiative (EII). The initiative aims to democratize access to the internet by training neighborhood leaders known as Digital Stewards to design and deploy community wireless networks. The DCTP emphasizes access, participation, common ownership, and healthy communities, as outlined in the Detroit Digital Justice Principles.

Problem Statement

Digital Stewards play a crucial role in bridging the digital divide within Detroit. However, they face the challenge of learning DCTP content in an engaging and accessible manner, ultimately demystifying technology use within their communities.

Solution

To address this challenge, the solution is to provide potential Digital Stewards with an online tool that contains DCTP curriculum and activities. This tool will make it more convenient for Digital Stewards to attend DCTP workshops, offering asynchronous learning opportunities. This approach will lead to increased enrollment, time and cost savings, and, ultimately, contribute to expanding internet accessibility across Detroit.

Challenges
  1. Instructor Training: Instructors accustomed to traditional classrooms will need to undergo training to effectively use the new online tool.
  2. Testing Constraints: Due to a tight timeline, prototype testing was not conducted; instead, the tool's development was handed over to DCTP.
  3. Resource Limitations: Limited resources within the DCTP team necessitated working with existing features rather than introducing many new elements to the web tool.

Development Process

  • 1

    Discover

    In this phase, the focus was on understanding how Digital Stewards are onboarded to the program, how they participate in both in-person and virtual workshops, and how they engage with lesson content and teachers.

  • 2

    Ideate

    The ideation phase involved brainstorming different ways of delivering the digital curriculum. The initial approach involved gamifying lesson content, where individuals had specific roles and responsibilities within activities. This approach evolved into a slide format to fit lesson content and maximize the use of the tool's features.

  • 3

    Design

    During the design phase, online curriculum prototypes were developed. This included determining how content should be adapted and how activities should be delivered in an online format.

  • 4

    Listen

    Throughout the design process, stakeholders were actively engaged for feedback on all prototype versions. However, due to time constraints, user testing of the finalized prototypes was not conducted.

Impact

The new online tool, designed to facilitate Digital Steward training, has the potential to significantly enhance the reach and effectiveness of the DCTP's Equitable Internet Initiative. It aims to democratize access to the internet and bridge the digital divide in Detroit, ultimately contributing to more accessible, inclusive, and equitable technology usage within the community.

Future Steps

To further improve the online tool, it is recommended to conduct user testing to identify any usability issues or areas for enhancement. Regular feedback loops with Digital Stewards and instructors can help tailor the tool to better meet their needs. Additionally, ongoing support and training for instructors will be essential to ensure a smooth transition to the online learning environment. With these steps in place, the DCTP can continue working towards its goal of making the internet more accessible and equitable for Detroit's underserved communities.
