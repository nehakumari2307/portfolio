Detroit Community Technology Project

Visual and Interaction Design Project

Background

Statistically, Detroit tops the list of “worst-connected cities” nationally, with nearly 60% of its residents lacking in-home broadband subscriptions, and 40% lacking any connection and 38% of residents live below the federal poverty level. The Detroit Community Technology Project (DCTP) aims to democratize access to the internet in underserved Detroit neighborhoods through the Equitable Internet Initiative (EII). The program trains neighborhood leaders (Digital Stewards) in designing and deploying community wireless networks with a commitment to the Detroit Digital Justice Principles, emphasizing access, participation, common ownership, and healthy communities. Through these Digital Stewards, EII addresses the staggering digital inequality within the city of Detroit.

Problem Statement

Digital Stewards need a way to learn DCTP content in an online, interactive, accessible, and engaging way that will result in demystifying technology use within the community.

Solution

Potential Digital Stewards will be able to attend DCTP workshops more conveniently by utilizing an online tool that contains curriculum and activities. This will result in more Digital Stewards enrollments, allow asynchronous delivery, save time and money, and ultimately move DCTP closer to enabling internet accessibility across Detroit.

Challenges
  1. 1. In order to deliver classroom content online, instructors who are used to synchronized classrooms will now undergo new training to learn this new tool.
  2. 2. Considering limited resources in DCTP's team, it was not possible to introduce many features to the web tool instead, the design had to be constructed around existing features.
  3. 3. Testing the prototypes developed for the tool was not carried out due to a short timeline but rather was handed over to DCTP.

Development Process

  • 1

    Discover

    Focus on how the Digital Stewards are onboarded to the program and how they undergo in-person & virtual workshops. Learn the lesson content and interact with teachers to understand how they deliver activities related to each lesson.

  • 2

    Ideate

    Brainstormed different ways of delivering and articulated the digital curriculum. The first approach was a gamified lesson content where each individual had a specific role and responsibility to complete the activity. The first iteration was modified to fit the lesson content in a slide format and design activities using the tool's feature.

  • 3

    Design

    Modeled online curriculum prototypes, including how content should be modified, and how activities should be adapted online.

  • 4

    Listen

    Throughout the design process, stakeholders were engaged for feedback on all prototype versions. Due to time constraint, user testing of finalized prototypes were not carried out.
