Optimizing User Experience for LSA Transfer Bridges Program

Background

The University of Michigan's LSA Transfer Bridges program is dedicated to supporting prospective students from Michigan community colleges seeking to transfer to U-M Ann Arbor's College of Literature, Science, and the Arts (LSA) for their undergraduate degrees. The program offers individualized support, academic advising, social experiences, and connections to U-M faculty. The overarching vision is to expand outreach by reaching more community colleges across Michigan and growing the program in the coming years.

Problem Statement

Address the challenges of increasing outreach to more community college students by enhancing the workflow of the Transfer Bridges team.

Challenges of Current System
  1. 1. Budget Constraints: Limited financial resources made it challenging for the program to hire additional advisors and program managers, leading to advisors taking on project management roles, which further strained their capacity.
  2. 2. Overwhelmed Program Staff: The team frequently grappled with an overwhelming volume of daily emails from students inquiring about the transfer process, prerequisites, advising slot bookings, and more.
  3. 3. Virtual Engagement: The transition to virtual sessions from in-person workshops held at various Michigan community colleges resulted in a diminished experience, impacting the program's ability to attract potential students.
Solution
In response to these challenges, the following solutions were proposed to improve the outreach and workflow:
  • 1. FAQ Autoreply Email Template: The implementation of an autoreply email system featuring a frequently asked questions (FAQ) template. This template would address the most common queries, alleviating the strain on program staff and offering quicker responses to students.
  • 2. Video Snippets: The addition of video snippets to the program's website homepage. These videos would comprehensively explain the transfer process, requirements, and how students can effectively utilize the program, ensuring that vital information is readily accessible to prospective students.
  • 4. Expand Virtual Engagement: To compensate for the diminished in-person experience, consider enhancing virtual engagement options, such as webinars, interactive Q&A sessions, and virtual campus tours. These initiatives will make the program more attractive and informative for prospective students.
  • 3. CRM Software Integration: Implementing customer relationship management (CRM) software to automate tracking and follow-up tasks for potential students.

Development Process

  • 1

    Background Research

    The project began with a comprehensive review of research articles to gain an understanding of the transfer process for students moving from community colleges to universities in the United States. This research explored the underlying purpose, benefits, and requirements necessary for a successful transfer.

  • 2

    Client Interview

    Six interviews were conducted, involving key members of the Transfer Bridges program team, including the Manager/Project Coordinator, Advisor, and current transfer students. These interviews aimed to understand the existing workflow for communicating with prospective students and within the program team.

  • 3

    Affinity Wall

    The affinity wall methodology was employed to analyze data collected from the interviews. This process helped organize and consolidate information, shedding light on the existing workflow challenges and pinpointing the core problem areas.

  • 4

    Key Findings

    The research identified key findings, highlighting significant obstacles to expanding the program to multiple community colleges. The "Information gap" between the program, prospective students, and within the program's internal communication emerged as a key challenge.

  • 5

    Recommendations

    Recommendations given to the client included incorporating a video on the website that talks about requirements for transfer, how to navigate the process, and answers a few general questions. Another recommendation is to integrate CRM software which helps reducing the manual work of tracking and following up on potential students. Lastly, have an automated email when students sign-up for an advising session with resources and common FAQs to prepare students beforehand.

Impact
The efforts to elevate user experience and accessibility on the Choose Improve platform have already yielded several impactful outcomes:
  1. 1. Enhanced Efficiency: The introduction of an autoreply email system with a FAQ template has significantly reduced the workload on program staff. They can now provide quick and consistent responses to common inquiries, resulting in more efficient communication with prospective students.
  2. 2. Improved Access to Information: The video snippets on the program's website have made essential information more accessible to prospective transfer students. This has not only reduced the information gap but has also increased user engagement and understanding of the transfer process.
  3. 3. Cost-Efficiency: By automating key communication processes, the program has achieved cost savings by reducing the need for additional hires. The program can now operate more efficiently with its existing resources.
Future Steps
To build on the current successes and continue enhancing the LSA Transfer Bridges Program's outreach and workflow, the following future steps have been identified:
  1. 1. Continue to gather user feedback and insights to make iterative improvements to the website's content, including the FAQ template and video snippets. This ongoing process will ensure that the information remains up-to-date and relevant.
  2. 2. Develop additional video resources and written content that delve deeper into specific aspects of the transfer process. Providing comprehensive and detailed information will help prospective students make well-informed decisions.
  3. 3. Develop an online community or forum where prospective and current transfer students can connect, share their experiences, and seek advice. This will foster a sense of belonging and provide valuable peer-to-peer support.
