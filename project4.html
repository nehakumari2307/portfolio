LSA Transfer Bridges Program

Contextual Inquiry Project

Background

UM's LSA's Transfer Bridges program helps prospective students from Michigan community colleges who want to transfer to U-M Ann Arborâ€™s LSA for their undergraduate degree. The program offers individual support and advising, social experiences, and connections to the U-M faculty to prospective transfer students. Their vision is to increase their outreach by covering more community colleges across Michigan and expand their program in upcoming years.

Problem Statement

Increase outreach to more community college students by improving the workflow of the transfer bridges team.

Challenges of Current System
  1. 1. A lack of budget has made it difficult for the transfer program to hire new advisors and program managers, which often led to advisors taking on project manager roles.
  2. 2. Program Staff often overstretch as they receive too many daily emails from students on queries related to the process, the prereqs, advising slot booking, etc.
  3. 3. Virtual sessions lacked the experience of in-person workshops conducted at different Michigan community colleges that attracted more potential students to enroll for this program.
Solution

With all the constraints in mind, we recommend automating most of the workflow, which includes an autoreply email FAQ template that answers the most popular and frequently asked questions. Another solution was to add video snippets on the website's home page that explained the process, the requirements, and how to utilize this program.

Development Process

  • 1

    Background Research

    Reading research articles to understand how transferring students from community college to university work in the United States, its underlying purpose, benefits, and requirements to make this transfer successful.

  • 2

    Client Interview

    To understand the current workflow the Transfer Bridges program team uses to communicate with prospective students and within themselves, six interviews were conducted that included the Manager/Project Coordinator, Advisor, and current set of transfer students.

  • 3

    Affinity Wall

    Affinity wall methodology was used to analyze data points collected from interviews. This process helped organize and consolidate information around the workflow that indirectly highlighted the underlying problem.

  • 4

    Key Findings

    The research identified key findings that will be obstacles to expanding the program to multiple community colleges. Due to limited staffing, the "Information gap" between the program, prospective students, and within program members is one of the most important finding.

  • 5

    Recommendations

    Recommendations given to the client included incorporating a video on the website that talks about requirements for transfer, how to navigate the process, and answers a few general questions. Another recommendation is to integrate CRM software which helps reducing the manual work of tracking and following up on potential students. Lastly, have an automated email when students sign-up for an advising session with resources and common FAQs to prepare students beforehand.
